Jupiter Simulation Project Proposal

Team Members: Zhenbang (Roger) Yu, Hanze (David) Tan, Jerry Li, Angel Aldaco

Summary

Our project aims to simulate the atmospheric conditions and surface features of Jupiter using computer graphics techniques. By creating a realistic representation of Jupiter, we hope to enhance understanding and appreciation of the largest planet in our solar system. The team consists of Roger Yu, David Tan, Jerry Li, and Angel Aldaco.

Problem Description

The lack of detailed, interactive simulations of Jupiter's atmosphere and surface limits our ability to study and explore the planet in depth. This project seeks to address this gap by developing a visually accurate and immersive simulation. Challenges include accurately modeling Jupiter's complex weather patterns and dynamic atmosphere.

Goals and Deliverables

Baseline Plan

  • Create a 3D model of Jupiter's surface and atmosphere through texture mapping.
  • Implement realistic weather effects such as cloud formations and storms.
  • Develop user controls for interacting(Light sources, etc) with the simulation.
  • Evaluate performance and optimize for smooth rendering.

Aspirational Plan

  • Incorporate real-time data to simulate current conditions on Jupiter.
  • Detailed the flow of the surface of this gas gaint.
  • Include self-rotation.
